About

A Life Dedicated to the Dhamma

From early ordination to scholarly pursuit and compassionate teaching

Bhante Aparekke Sirisumana, ordained as a Sri Lankan Buddhist monk in 1998, obtained higher ordination in 2007. He graduated from the University of Ruhuna with a B.A. in Buddhist Philosophy and also earned a second B.A. in Buddhist Studies from the Buddhist College of Singapore.

He completed his M.A. in Buddhist Studies at Mahachulalongkornrajavidyalaya University and a Diploma in Buddhist Counselling. Currently, he teaches Early Buddhism and Buddhism & Modern Issues at Mangala Vihara (Buddhist Temple), Singapore.

With decades of monastic practice and academic scholarship, Bhante Sumana bridges traditional Theravada wisdom with contemporary issues, guiding students and devotees toward a deeper understanding of the Dhamma.

Teachings & Scholarly Work

Sharing the Dhamma

Academic courses and spiritual guidance offered at Mangala Vihara

Early Buddhism

Exploring the original teachings of the Buddha as preserved in the Pāli Canon — the Suttas, Vinaya, and Abhidhamma — with emphasis on historical context, doctrinal analysis, and practical application to daily life.

Buddhism & Modern Issues

Examining how Buddhist principles address contemporary challenges — from environmental ethics and social justice to mental health, technology, and the intersection of ancient wisdom with modern scientific understanding.

Buddhist Counselling

Integrating traditional Buddhist psychology with counselling methodologies to offer compassionate, mindfulness-based guidance for individuals navigating life's difficulties and seeking inner peace.

Meditation Practice

Guiding practitioners through Samatha and Vipassanā meditation techniques rooted in the Theravada tradition, cultivating mindfulness, concentration, and insight on the path to liberation.
